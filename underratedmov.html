Underrated Movies You Need to Watch

Published on August 10, 2024

Introduction

In the vast world of cinema, there are many films that, despite their brilliance, have flown under the radar. These underrated movies may not have gained the widespread acclaim they deserve, but they offer unique storytelling, unforgettable performances, and cinematic experiences that rival even the biggest blockbusters. Here’s a list of underrated gems that every movie lover should watch.

1. The Fall (2006)

Genre: Fantasy, Adventure
Director: Tarsem Singh

Why You Should Watch It: The Fall is a visually stunning masterpiece that weaves together a tale of fantasy and reality. The film's breathtaking cinematography and captivating performances make it an underrated gem that deserves more recognition.

Image credit: source

2. The Secret of Kells (2009)

Genre: Animation, Fantasy
Directors: Tomm Moore, Nora Twomey

Why You Should Watch It: This Irish animated film is a visual treat with its intricate art style and folklore-inspired story. Despite being nominated for an Academy Award, it remains lesser-known compared to other animated features.

Image credit: source

3. Primer (2004)

Genre: Sci-Fi, Thriller
Director: Shane Carruth

Why You Should Watch It: Primer is an indie sci-fi film that challenges the mind with its complex narrative. Made on a shoestring budget, it's a testament to how creativity can overcome financial constraints in filmmaking.

Image credit: source

4. Moon (2009)

Genre: Sci-Fi, Drama
Director: Duncan Jones

Why You Should Watch It: Moon is a thought-provoking sci-fi film that delves into themes of isolation and identity. Sam Rockwell delivers a standout performance in this emotionally resonant story.

Image credit: source

5. A Ghost Story (2017)

Genre: Drama, Fantasy
Director: David Lowery

Why You Should Watch It: A Ghost Story offers a unique and contemplative take on life, death, and time. Its minimalist approach and haunting visuals make it a deeply moving experience.

Image credit: source

6. The Nice Guys (2016)

Genre: Action, Comedy
Director: Shane Black

Why You Should Watch It: This buddy-cop comedy combines sharp writing with fantastic performances from Russell Crowe and Ryan Gosling. It's an entertaining and stylish throwback to 1970s detective stories.

Image credit: source

7. Under the Silver Lake (2018)

Genre: Mystery, Thriller
Director: David Robert Mitchell

Why You Should Watch It: Under the Silver Lake is a quirky and complex neo-noir film with a puzzling narrative and an intriguing mystery. Its unique style and storytelling make it a cult favorite.

Image credit: source

8. The Lobster (2015)

Genre: Comedy, Drama
Director: Yorgos Lanthimos

Why You Should Watch It: The Lobster offers a darkly comedic and satirical look at relationships and societal norms. Its originality and offbeat humor make it a memorable watch.

Image credit: source

9. The Witch (2015)

Genre: Horror, Drama
Director: Robert Eggers

Why You Should Watch It: This atmospheric horror film is set in 1630s New England and provides a chilling exploration of fear and paranoia. Its authenticity and unsettling tension set it apart from typical horror films.

Image credit: source

10. Colossal (2016)

Genre: Sci-Fi, Comedy
Director: Nacho Vigalondo

Why You Should Watch It: Colossal blends sci-fi with dark comedy in an inventive story about a woman who discovers she has a mysterious connection to a giant monster. It’s both entertaining and thought-provoking.

Image credit: source

These films may not have topped the box office or become household names, but they offer rich, rewarding experiences for those willing to give them a chance. Whether you're a fan of fantasy, animation, or thought-provoking sci-fi, there's something on this list for everyone. So, grab some popcorn and enjoy these underrated cinematic treasures.
